Aaron Judge is one of Major League Baseball's premier power hitters, playing right field for the New York Yankees. Born on April 26, 1992, Judge was selected by the Yankees in the first round of the 2013 MLB Draft. His rookie season in 2017 was historic, as he hit 52 home runs, breaking the rookie record previously held by Mark McGwire, and winning the American League Rookie of the Year award. Known for his towering stature (6'7") and immense strength, Judge has continued to be a key player for the Yankees, earning multiple All-Star selections and Silver Slugger Awards. In 2022, he made history by breaking the American League single-season home run record with 62 home runs, surpassing Roger Maris.

Giannis Antetokounmpo, known as the "Greek Freak," is a dominant force in the NBA, playing for the Milwaukee Bucks. Born on December 6, 1994, in Athens, Greece, to Nigerian parents, Giannis’ journey to NBA stardom is one of perseverance and hard work. Drafted 15th overall in the 2013 NBA Draft, Giannis quickly developed into one of the league’s top players. Known for his incredible versatility, length, and athleticism, he plays both forward and center. Giannis led the Bucks to their first NBA championship in 50 years in 2021, earning Finals MVP honors. He is also a two-time NBA MVP (2019, 2020) and widely regarded as one of the best players in the world.

Patrick Mahomes is the star quarterback for the Kansas City Chiefs and one of the NFL's most electrifying talents. Born on September 17, 1995, Mahomes played college football at Texas Tech University before being selected by the Chiefs in the first round of the 2017 NFL Draft. Known for his strong arm, mobility, and playmaking ability, Mahomes became the Chiefs' starting quarterback in 2018 and won the NFL MVP award in his first season as a starter. In 2020, he led the Chiefs to a Super Bowl victory and was named Super Bowl MVP. Mahomes is widely regarded as one of the faces of the NFL and continues to redefine the quarterback position with his dynamic style of play.

Shohei Ohtani is a once-in-a-generation talent from Japan who has made waves in Major League Baseball as both a dominant pitcher and a powerful hitter. Born on July 5, 1994, Ohtani joined the Los Angeles Angels in 2018 after a successful career in Japan’s Nippon Professional Baseball. His dual abilities as a pitcher and designated hitter have led him to comparisons with the legendary Babe Ruth. Ohtani was named the American League MVP in 2021 after an extraordinary season where he excelled on both sides of the ball. His remarkable blend of skills and humble demeanor have earned him global recognition, making him one of the most exciting and marketable players in the sport.
